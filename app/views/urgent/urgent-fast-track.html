1 week Fast Track

Take your old passport and any documents to a Post Office.

Staff at the Post Office will help you apply and pay for your passport, and book an appointment at a passport office.

You’ll collect your new passport at your appointment.

What it costs

It costs:

  • £142 or an adult passport (or £152 or a 50 page frequent traveller passport)
  • £122 for a child passport (or £152 or a 50 page frequent traveller passport)

What you’ll need to take to the Post Office

What you need to take depends on what type of application you’re making.

If you’re renewing or replacing a passport

You must take your old passport to the Post Office if you still have it.

If you’ve changed your name, you’ll also need to bring proof of your new name (like a marriage certificate)

If your passport is lost, you’ll need the name and email address of someone who can confirm your identity.

If you’re applying for someone under 16

You must take their old passport to the Post Office if they have one.

You’ll need to give both of their parents’ details, including their:

  • names, dates and places of birth
  • nationality
  • UK passport details, if they have them
  • details of their marriage or civil partnership if it applies

If you cannot provide both parents’ details, you need to say why, for example you’re the only parent named on the birth certificate.

You’ll also need the email address of someone who can confirm the child’s identity, unless you’re renewing a child’s passport who is 12 or over.

Your passport office appointment

You’ll collect your new passport at your appointment.

You can ask someone else to go to your appointment for you. They’ll need to bring:

  • a signed and dated letter from you, naming them and giving them permission to collect the passport
  • their ID or example a passport or driving licence

Changing your appointment

Call the passport adviceline to reschedule your appointment.
